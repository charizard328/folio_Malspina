Exposed plasticity

Project Info

A glacier is an aggregate of a few drops of water that has grown little by little over millions of years. It is still growing and has enormous and massive energy. This gigantic flow, basically a river-like motion, however, is gradual enough that erosion and sedimentation can not be compared with river action. Only records collected over decades will demonstrate the dynamics of the glacier.

In gigantic energy flows, glaciers have various properties in response to temperature, pressure, velocity, bedrock shape, and so on. It does not flow, it piles up, it flows down, it carries down the bedrock, and the friction force with the bedrock also develops the crevasse on the surface of the glacier. A glacier is both a solid and a flowing plastic.

This study of journey of glacier from a deep mountain range to reach sea level is a survey of various terrains that glaciers have or are exposed from glaciers.
